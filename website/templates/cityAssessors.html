City Assessor’s Department

The City Assessor’s Department is responsible for the valuation and management of rateable hereditaments within the city of Port of Spain, ensuring compliance with relevant acts and providing essential services to state agencies and the public.

Role and Functions

The role of the City Assessment Department is clearly expressed in Section 80 of Act 21 of 1990:

“The Assessor shall ascertain and assess the annual rateable value of each rateable hereditament within the city and record the names of owners, occupiers or tenants thereof and submit to Council for the fixing of the House Rate payable before March 31, each year.”

The core functions of the City Assessor’s Department are as follows:

  • Calculation and determination of Lease Rental for Corporation Properties.
  • Processing of Return of Ownership.
  • Valuation of Hereditaments within the city limits.
  • Numbering and re-numbering of hereditaments.
  • Mergers and sub-division of properties.
  • Preparing and maintenance of House Rate Books.
  • Preparing and Issuing of House Rate Notices.
  • Research Data Collection and Analysis/Market Intelligence.
  • Technical advice on Valuation Matters.
  • Servicing the public and other State Agencies such as WASA and T&TEC.

Departmental Information

The City Assessor’s Departmental staff comprises:

  • One (1) Deputy City Assessor (Ag.)
  • One (1) Valuation Assistant
  • One (1) Clerk II
  • One (1) Clerk/Typist
  • One (1) Female Labourer

Cemetery Allotments

Change of Name

Requirements:

  • Original and copy of Deed of Poll or Marriage Certificate.
  • Original Assignment Card/Grant.
  • Identification of applicant/owner.

Administrative cost: $57.50

Change of Ownership (Death, Inclusion, Transfer)

Requirements:

  • Original Assignment Card/Grant/Certified copy.
  • Original and copy of Certified Death Certificate.
  • Original and copies of Probate of Will and Will/Letters of Administration.
  • Statutory Declaration and Identification of applicant/owner.

Administrative cost: $57.50

Inclusion of Names

Requirements:

  • Completed 'Transfer of Allotment' Form.
  • Original Assignment Card/Grant/Certified Copy.
  • Original and copy of Certified Death Certificate.
  • Identification of applicant/owner.
  • Statutory Declaration.

Cost of transaction: $230.00 VAT inclusive

Transfer of Allotment

When allotment is sold or owner's name is removed:

  • Completed 'Transfer of Allotment' Form.
  • Original Assignment Card/Grant/Certified Copy.
  • Original and copies of Probate of Will and Letters of Administration.

Cost of transaction: $575.00 VAT inclusive

Sub-division/Merger of Allotments

Requirements:

  • Letter requesting Sub-division/Merger.
  • Original of Assignment Card/Grant/Certified Copy.

New grants issued at a cost of $57.50 VAT inclusive.

Exhumations

Requirements:

  • Completed application in duplicate, signed in the presence of a Commissioner of Affidavits.
  • A License approved by the Mayor granting permission for exhumation.

Cost of Exhumation:

  • Less than 6 years: $3,080.00
  • 6 to 12 years: $2,760.00
  • Over 12 years: $1,960.00
